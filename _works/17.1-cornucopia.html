Password for full video documentation: Cornucopia2023 brief: > The Gartenhaus of the Academy of Fine Arts in Munich is emptied out and dressed up as a room for potential happenings. Three ceramic elements (one staircase, one platform, one pedestal) mark the three floors of the house as stage sets. Objects and props lie around and build pictorial compositions, backgrounds to actions that are yet to happen. The rooms stay silent, loaded with visual hints and commentaries, offering themselves to the visitors like empty shells of Renaissance paintings. During the performance, 8 performers appropriate the space, moving between the 3 different stage sets and using the props. They perform a script which consists of 20 text fragments, each of them a scene, a visual poem, a pictorial composition in itself. The choir of voices shifts between synchronic, compact and polyphonic, asynchronic, fragmented; the group of performers being at times authoritarian choir, at times specific characters, or exchangeable units. With language broken down into grids, patterns and tropes, the performers become architectural elements to the house, as well as literature references and caricatures. They tell stories of homes, mythologies of interiors and everyday objects, shifting perspectives between the individual and the collective, protagonist and choir, foreground and background, human subject and object. ---
