Password for full video documentation: Cornucopia2023 brief: > Three ceramic elements (a staircase, a floor, a platform) mark three stage sets. Objects and props (coins, cutlery, glasses filled with water) lie around and form pictorial compositions, backgrounds for actions that have not yet taken place. The space remains silent, loaded with visual clues and comments, and offers itself to the visitors like empty shells of Renaissance paintings. During the performance, eight speakers appropriate the space, move between the ceramic stages and use the props. They recite a script consisting of 21 text fragments, each of them a visual poem, an image composition in its own right. The choir of voices alternates between synchronous, compact and polyphonic, asynchronous, scattered; the group is sometimes authoritative choir, sometimes specific characters, literary references or interchangeable architectural units. They tell stories of houses and interiors, mythologies of domesticity and everyday objects, shifting perspectives between individual and collective, foreground and background, human mass and displayed merchandise. The extremely fast and extremely slow pace of the script performed by single or multiple units (the performers as single characters or as a group) punctuate the exhibition room and build sound axes in space. ---
